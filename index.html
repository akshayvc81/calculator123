CALCULATOR

TRIVIA:-The abacus is an ancient calculating tool that uses beads, pebbles, or flat discs to perform arithmetic calculations by sliding them along rods, wires, or lines. The abacus may have originated in the Middle East before Christ, and the oldest counting boards have been found in fourth century BCE Rome. The invention of the abacus likely occurred in Sumeria around 2700 to 2300 B.C.E., which used a base 60 system. The oldest known example of an abacus is the Salamis Tablet from Greece (300 B.C.E).
